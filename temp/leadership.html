UB Hacking

I was a part of UB Hacking for 3 out of my 4 years in college. In my senior year, I worked as the director for the hackathon (2018 - 2019). As the director, I lead seven teams, overseeing the seamless organization and execution of a highly successful 48-hour tech hackathon attended by over 1,000 participants. I spearheaded communication efforts with corporate sponsors, securing approximately $40,000 in funding to support the event. Additionally, I coordinated over 100 volunteers and mentors ensured all requirements were met, from the planning stages to day-of tasks. Before becoming the director, I also lead the logistics and fun team (2017-2018) as well as volunteered as a member of the logistics team (2016-2017).

STEM DivCon

I founded the first student-run STEM Diversity Conference in collaboration with SWE, WiSE, SHPE, oSTEM, and Scientista, funded by UB Engineering Partners. I organized a 6-member Academic and a 6-member Industry Panel, providing minority STEM students the opportunity to ask questions and gain valuable tips.
